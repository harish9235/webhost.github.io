Q.1)
A man deposits 10,000 in a saving account an interest rate of 4% per annum. How much money will be have after 3 years if the interest is compounded annually
  1. Rs.8542
  2. Rs.11248.64
  3. Rs.5545
  4. Rs.4343
Answer :Option B

10,000+4% interest=10400….1st year
10,400+4% interest=10816….2nd year
10,816+4% interest=11248.64….3rd year
Therefore, A man will have Rs.11248.64 after 3 years
OR…..Using formula
A=P[1+(R/100)]n
A=10000[1+(4/100)]3
A=Rs.11248.64

Q.2)
The compound interest on Rs.30,000 at 7% per annum is Rs.4347. Calculate The period in years
  1. 3 Years
  2. 4 Years
  3. 2 Years
  4. 4 Years
Answer :Option C

1st year compound interest amount at 7% rate of interest with principal 30,000 is equal to 2100
2nd year compound interest amount=2247
So compound interest amount in two years=2100+2247=4347
Therefore,period is 2 years

Q.3)
What is the difference between the compound interests on Rs. 5000 for 1 & 1/2 years at 4% per annum compounded yearly and half-yearly?
  1. Rs.8.2
  2. Rs.2.04
  3. Rs.4.5
  4. Rs.4.3
Answer :Option B

C.I. when interest compounded yearly=[5000x(1+4/100)(1+1/2x4/100)]= Rs.5304
C.I. when interest is compounded half-yearly=rs.5000(1+2/100)3= Rs.5306.04
Difference = Rs.(5306.04-5304) = Rs.2.04

Q.4)
Find the compound interest on Rs. 16,000 at 20% per annum for 9 months, compounded quarterly
  1. Rs.8742
  2. Rs.1124
  3. Rs.5545
  4. Rs.2525
Answer :Option D

Principal = Rs.16000; Time = 9 months =3 quarters;
Rate = 20% per annum = 5% per quarter
Amount =[16000 x (1+(5/100))3] = Rs.18522
CI = Rs. (18522 - 16000) = Rs. 2522
