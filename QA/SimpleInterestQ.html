Q.1)
The rate at which sum becomes four times of itself in 25 years at simple Interest will be
  1. 30%
  2. 12%
  3. 27%
  4. 42%
Answer :Option B

Let, p=100, T=25 years, 4 times in 25 years=400
So Interest amount =400-100=300
Simple Interest=[p(principal)xTxR(Interest rate)]/100
300=(100x25xR)/100
Therefore, The rate at which sum becomes four times of itself in 25 years at simple Interest will be 12%

Q.5)
A sum becomes five times of itself in 20 years. It will become 7 times itself in how many years?
  1. 32 Years
  2. 40 Years
  3. 30 Years
  4. 42 Years
Answer :Option C

Interest earned =P×r×t
P= Principal amount
r= Rate of interest
t= Time period
Here, sum becomes 5 times in 20 years,
⇒ Interest earned 4 times the principal amount.
4P = P×r×20100
r = (420)×100 = 20%
here we need to calculate for 7 times of the principal, interest should be 600%
i.e P+6P = 7P
Time = (600%)/(20%)
Time = 30 years
